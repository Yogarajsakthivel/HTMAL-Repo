Traditional Photography

Uses a photographic film, strip or sheet of transparent plastic film base coated on one side with a gelatin emulsion containing microscopically small light-sensitive silver halide crystals. It involves a photographic processing in which the photographic film and paper is treated.

Candit Photography

They call it candid photography and traditional regular photography. In candid photography, where the subject is unaware and does not pose. Whereas in traditional photography, people sit, pose, and smile for the camera. Once the picture is clicked, they go back to their natural best.


Traditional Videography

A Traditional videographer has a typical video camera usually shoots continuously. There’s less creativity involved besides documenting everything that’s being done. There’s no storytelling involved and it doesn’t give you those emotions you would get from a Cinematic Wedding.


Candid Videography

Candid, the term itself means frank, open and honest. Candid photography is all about capturing photographs in their most natural setting, without asking the bride, groom or the guests to pose for the shot. It brings in a real sense of spontaneity and eliminates the possibility of the persons becoming too conscious.
